Getting a Sunflower Bouquet? Here's What You Should Know

Sunflowers are cheery, vibrant, and lively blooms. The bright sunflower is known worldwide for its beauty and allure, but it also stands for loyalty and longevity. Sunflower bouquets or arrangements will be the ideal choice for your floral needs, whether for home decor or private gifting. Since this flower is often connected to happiness, it will be appropriate as a gift for diverse events, such as graduation ceremonies, birthday parties, and others.
Thinking about sunflowers makes you feel happy, comfortable, and nostalgic no matter where your thoughts are taking you. You can choose the flower assortment style depending on the occasion or event. For instance, if you want a bouquet for a birthday gift with a more blossoming look, choose a bouquet of medium-sized sunflowers. Let's read on to understand more about this flower.


Interesting facts about Sunflowers

Sunflowers have more to offer than simply lovely appearances. Its value and beauty speak for themselves. Don't you believe it? Read the following amazing facts about the sunflower, and you will:

  • One sunflower contains thousands of tiny flowers and approximately 2000 seeds.
  • It grew over 5000 years ago in North America.
  • Young blossoms demonstrate heliotropism behavior, turning their curved necks wherever the sun goes during the day, from east to west.
  • It is a multipurpose plant that can produce oil. Its seeds are also used as a healthy snack and nutritious food for birds.
  • The sunflower is a symbol of warmth, positivity, and rebirth.


How to make a sunflower bouquet last longer

If you think that taking care of sunflowers is similar to other flowers, you are somewhat correct. There are just a few exceptions when you care for a sunflower bouquet. If someone has given you sunflowers as a gift and you don't want to them droop early, follow the following instructions to make them last for more than a week


  • Put them in a clean vase filled with hot water

    • Take a tall vase and pour some hot water into it. Now cut the stems of the sunflower from an angle of 45 degrees from the bottom, about an inch. Remove the leaves from the bottom of the stem to avoid bacteria growth. You can also drop a penny into the vase to prevent bacteria from growing.


  • Sunlight exposure is a must

    • Sunflowers love sunshine. Put your sunflower-filled vase in sunlight for a few hours throughout the day. It will make them look supercharged and fresh.


  • Recondition your sunflowers every day

    • With sunflowers, you need to be extra cautious compared to other flowers. They require more attention. You should change the water in the vase and re-trim their stems every day to make them look lively and vibrant as they are.
